Key Features for Attrition and Strategies to mitigate them !!!

Employees who are required to work overtime consistently may experience burnout and a decrease in job satisfaction. Organizations can reduce attrition by implementing more efficient work schedules, promoting work-life balance, and offering additional support to employees with heavy workloads.

Employees with longer tenures often have a stronger commitment to the organization. Encouraging professional development, career growth, and internal promotions can help increase employees' total working years with the company.

Higher job levels often come with increased responsibilities and better compensation. To reduce attrition, companies can establish clear career paths, mentorship programs, and offer opportunities for employees to advance within the organization.

Adequate compensation is a crucial factor in retaining employees. To minimize attrition, companies should conduct regular salary reviews and ensure that employees are compensated competitively based on their roles and industry standards.

Younger employees might be more prone to exploring other opportunities, while older employees might seek stability. To address this, organizations can design tailored retention strategies for different age groups, such as offering mentoring programs for younger employees and retirement planning for older ones.
